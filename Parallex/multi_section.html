Cooking In Brass Utensils

Research suggests that brass may contribute to the release of trace elements such as zinc and copper, which are vital for various physiological functions in the human body. The heat conductivity of brass ensures that food is cooked evenly, preserving its nutritional integrity.

Health Benefits

Brass consists of 70 percent copper and 30 percent zinc, the properties of which are extremely important to improve your health. Cooking and eating with brassware is beneficial because it loses only 7 percent of the nutritional value of your food, thereby making it healthier.
